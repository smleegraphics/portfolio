Blender is a powerful, free, and open-source 3D creation suite. If you're new to 3D modeling, it can seem overwhelming at first, but with the right approach, you'll be creating amazing work in no time.

## Why Blender?

1. **Free and Open Source** - No subscription fees or licensing costs
2. **Full-Featured** - Modeling, sculpting, texturing, animation, rendering, and more
3. **Active Community** - Tons of tutorials and resources available
4. **Industry Standard** - Used in professional production pipelines

## Getting Started

### The Interface

When you first open Blender, you'll see the default scene with a cube, camera, and light. The interface is divided into several areas:

- **3D Viewport** - Where you see and manipulate your scene
- **Outliner** - Lists all objects in your scene
- **Properties** - Settings for objects, materials, and rendering
- **Timeline** - For animation

### Essential Shortcuts

Here are the most important keyboard shortcuts to learn:

- `G` - Grab/Move
- `R` - Rotate
- `S` - Scale
- `Tab` - Toggle Edit Mode
- `Shift + A` - Add new object
- `X` - Delete

## Next Steps

Once you're comfortable with the basics, I recommend exploring:

1. Modifier workflows
2. UV unwrapping and texturing
3. Basic materials and shading
4. Simple animations

Happy modeling!
